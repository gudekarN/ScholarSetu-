Aadhaar Linking vs DBT — What's the Difference?

Students often confuse Aadhaar–Bank linking with DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer) enabling. Both are required for your scholarship to reach your account — but they are two completely separate steps done in different places.

🚪
Aadhaar–Bank Linking
Connecting your Aadhaar number to your savings account so the bank recognises you as the account holder.
  • Done at your bank branch, ATM, or net banking
  • Links your 12-digit Aadhaar to the account number
  • Required for any government scheme — not just scholarships
  • One-time setup; doesn't expire unless you change banks
  • Verify status at UIDAI website or your bank app
⚡
DBT Enabling (NPCI Mapper)
Activating your Aadhaar in the NPCI mapper so scholarship funds can be routed directly to your account via the Aadhaar Payment Bridge.
  • Done via MahaDBT portal or your bank — separate from linking
  • Activates the Aadhaar Payment Bridge (APB) for your account
  • Without this, scholarship payment fails even if Aadhaar is linked
  • Must be active before submitting the scholarship form
  • Check status on NPCI website using your Aadhaar number
💡
In simple words: Aadhaar linking tells the bank "this account belongs to me." DBT enabling tells the government "send money to this Aadhaar-linked account." You need both. Many students have Aadhaar linked but DBT not enabled — their scholarship payment silently fails every year.
